# Veteran Flashcards

## Card 1

**Q:** What is the minimum active duty service requirement for a veteran to qualify for the VA Aid and Attendance benefit?

**A:** At least 90 days of active duty, with at least one day during an eligible period of war.

---

## Card 2

**Q:** Which wartime period for Aid and Attendance eligibility spans from August 5, 1964, to May 7, 1975?

**A:** The Vietnam War Era (for veterans who served anywhere in the world).

---

## Card 3

**Q:** Under what condition can a veteran younger than 65 qualify for Aid and Attendance?

**A:** They must be totally and permanently disabled or a resident in a skilled nursing facility.

---

## Card 4

**Q:** What are the five specific activities of daily living (ADLs) recognized by the VA?

**A:** Bathing, mobility/transferring, dressing, toileting, and eating.

---

## Card 5

**Q:** Define 'standby assistance' in the context of Aid and Attendance ADLs.

**A:** Having another person present within arm's reach to prevent injury during a daily living activity.

---

## Card 6

**Q:** How does the VA define 'custodial care' for pension eligibility?

**A:** Assistance with at least two ADLs or the requirement of $24/7$ supervision due to cognitive impairment.

---

## Card 7

**Q:** What is the official document name for a veteran's Report of Separation required for benefit applications?

**A:** DD Form 214.

---

## Card 8

**Q:** As of 2026, what is the approximate net worth limit for Aid and Attendance eligibility?

**A:** $\$163,699$.

---

## Card 9

**Q:** How long is the VA's look-back period for asset transfers regarding pension eligibility?

**A:** Three years.

---

## Card 10

**Q:** What requirement must a resident of an assisted living facility meet to qualify for Aid and Attendance?

**A:** They must be paying for and receiving assistance with at least two types of daily living activities.

---

## Card 11

**Q:** What happens to the Aid and Attendance benefit amount if a recipient is approved for Medicaid long-term care?

**A:** The benefit is reduced to approximately $\$90$ per month.

---

## Card 12

**Q:** How does Social Security income affect the calculation of Aid and Attendance benefits?

**A:** It is counted as income when determining eligibility and the final benefit amount.

---

## Card 13

**Q:** Explain the tax status of Combat-Related Special Compensation (CRSC).

**A:** It is a non-taxable special compensation for combat-related disabilities.

---

## Card 14

**Q:** Unlike CRSC, Concurrent Retirement and Disability Pay (CRDP) is considered _____ for tax purposes.

**A:** Taxable income (recoupment of retired pay).

---

## Card 15

**Q:** What is the minimum VA disability rating required for automatic enrollment in CRDP?

**A:** $50 \%$.

---

## Card 16

**Q:** What is the minimum VA disability rating required to apply for CRSC?

**A:** $10 \%$.

---

## Card 17

**Q:** How often can a military retiree switch between CRSC and CRDP programs?

**A:** Once per year during an annual 'program election' period.

---

## Card 18

**Q:** Disabilities resulting from 'instrumentalities of war' are specific criteria for which benefit?

**A:** Combat-Related Special Compensation (CRSC).

---

## Card 19

**Q:** Describe the 'VA waiver' or 'VA offset' mandated by law for military retirees.

**A:** Retirees must waive a portion of their DoD retired pay equal to the amount of their VA disability compensation.

---

## Card 20

**Q:** What causes retroactive debits and credits in a veteran's retired pay account when a disability rating changes?

**A:** The time lag in reporting and coordination between the VA and DFAS.

---

## Card 21

**Q:** According to federal retirement guidelines, when should an employee first request an estimate of their retirement annuity?

**A:** Five years before the proposed retirement date.

---

## Card 22

**Q:** What is the 'Five-Year Test' for continuing Federal Employees Health Benefits (FEHB) into retirement?

**A:** The employee must be continuously enrolled in FEHB for the five years of service immediately preceding retirement.

---

## Card 23

**Q:** How does the waiver authority for the FEHB five-year rule differ from the FEGLI rule?

**A:** OPM can waive the FEHB requirement for exceptional circumstances, but there is no waiver authority for FEGLI.

---

## Card 24

**Q:** Which federal retirement system (CSRS or FERS) allows voluntary annuities to begin on the first, second, or third day of the month?

**A:** Civil Service Retirement System (CSRS).

---

## Card 25

**Q:** When does a FERS voluntary retirement annuity typically begin?

**A:** On the first day of the month following retirement.

---

## Card 26

**Q:** What is the 'Sequence of Returns Risk' in retirement planning?

**A:** The risk that negative market returns early in retirement will disproportionately deplete a portfolio used for income.

---

## Card 27

**Q:** In a retirement 'bucket strategy,' what is the purpose of the first bucket (Liquidity)?

**A:** To cover day-to-day needs for the first $3$ to $5$ years using cash-equivalent, low-risk investments.

---

## Card 28

**Q:** The 'Lifestyle' bucket in a retirement strategy typically covers which timeframe?

**A:** Years $3$ through $10$ of retirement.

---

## Card 29

**Q:** What is the primary benefit of a 'bucket strategy' regarding market volatility?

**A:** It avoids the need to sell fluctuating assets (like stocks) during a down market to meet immediate income needs.

---

## Card 30

**Q:** Why is it mandatory for military retirees with TRICARE to enroll in Medicare Parts A and B?

**A:** To maintain health coverage through the TRICARE for Life program.

---

## Card 31

**Q:** How does Medicare Part B coordination work for a GEHA PPO plan holder?

**A:** Medicare becomes the primary insurance, often resulting in waived deductibles and copayments for the secondary FEHB plan.

---

## Card 32

**Q:** How are military retirement pensions generally treated for federal income tax purposes?

**A:** They are taxed as ordinary income.

---

## Card 33

**Q:** Name three states that have no state income tax as of 2026.

**A:** Any three from: Alaska, Florida, Nevada, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Washington, or Wyoming.

---

## Card 34

**Q:** How does living in a state that fully exempts military retirement pay affect a veteran's debt-to-income (DTI) ratio?

**A:** It lowers the DTI ratio by increasing net usable income, thereby increasing home-buying power.

---

## Card 35

**Q:** The Survivor Benefit Plan (SBP) provides a monthly payout equal to what percentage of the selected base amount?

**A:** $55 \%$.

---

## Card 36

**Q:** How does SBP coverage protect against 'longevity risk' for a beneficiary?

**A:** It provides a guaranteed monthly payout for the entire lifetime of the beneficiary, regardless of how long they live.

---

## Card 37

**Q:** What is the 'paid-up' status for SBP premiums?

**A:** Premiums stop after $30$ years of payments AND reaching age $70$.

---

## Card 38

**Q:** Why is the SBP decision considered a 'one-time irrevocable' choice?

**A:** It must be made at retirement and, with very few exceptions, cannot be changed or cancelled later.

---

## Card 39

**Q:** How does VA Disability compensation interact with Social Security retirement benefits?

**A:** They are independent; receiving VA disability does not reduce or offset Social Security retirement checks.

---

## Card 40

**Q:** What is the main exception where Social Security income *does* affect a VA benefit?

**A:** VA Pension (the needs-based program for wartime veterans).

---

## Card 41

**Q:** What is the submission window for a Benefits Delivery at Discharge (BDD) claim?

**A:** Between $90$ and $180$ days before the date of separation.

---

## Card 42

**Q:** For veterans who served between 1957 and 2001, what 'extra earnings' does Social Security add to their record?

**A:** Up to $\$1,200$ a year in additional earnings credits for military service.

---

## Card 43

**Q:** Which VA priority group includes veterans with a $100 \%$ disability rating?

**A:** Priority Group One.

---

## Card 44

**Q:** At what age can a veteran begin claiming reduced Social Security retirement benefits?

**A:** Age $62$.

---

## Card 45

**Q:** What is the 'standard' full retirement age for Social Security for those currently approaching retirement?

**A:** $66$ or $67$, depending on the birth year.

---

## Card 46

**Q:** What requirement must a surviving spouse meet regarding marriage duration for Aid and Attendance?

**A:** They must have been married to the veteran for at least one year before the veteran died (or had a child together).

---

## Card 47

**Q:** If a veteran enters active duty after September 7, 1980, what is the minimum length of service for pension eligibility?

**A:** $24$ months or the full period called to duty, with at least one day during wartime.

---

## Card 48

**Q:** Term: Countable Income (VA Pension)

**A:** Definition: Gross income minus specific deductible medical and care expenses used to determine financial eligibility.

---

## Card 49

**Q:** Under the 'Lifestyle' bucket, how is the 'Liquidity' bucket typically replenished?

**A:** By regularly shifting funds from the disciplined asset allocation of the lifestyle bucket into cash-equivalent assets.

---

## Card 50

**Q:** What happens to a veteran's Aid and Attendance eligibility if they receive a 'Dishonorable' discharge?

**A:** They are disqualified from most VA benefits, including Aid and Attendance.

---

## Card 51

**Q:** How is 'eating' defined as an ADL for VA purposes?

**A:** Helping someone eat, including cutting up food or feeding the person directly.

---

## Card 52

**Q:** What is the purpose of the Medicare General Enrollment Period (GEP)?

**A:** It allows individuals who missed their Initial Enrollment Period to sign up between Jan 1 and March 31.

---

## Card 53

**Q:** Why is VA drug coverage often considered less flexible than Medicare Part D?

**A:** It requires authorization by a VA provider and must be filled through a VA pharmacy or their mail-order system.

---

## Card 54

**Q:** In SBP planning, what happens to the coverage if the beneficiary dies before the retiree?

**A:** The coverage stops, and there are typically no refunds for premiums already paid.

---

## Card 55

**Q:** Which form is used for the SBP election by a military retiree?

**A:** DD Form 2656.

---

## Card 56

**Q:** How does the 'Social Security Fairness Act' generally relate to disability benefits according to the sources?

**A:** It is a topic for veterans to monitor regarding how various benefits might be calculated or adjusted.

---

## Card 57

**Q:** What is the impact of a 'high property tax' state on a retiree's VA loan affordability compared to income tax?

**A:** Property taxes often create a larger long-term financial hit and increase monthly escrow payments more than state income tax.

---

## Card 58

**Q:** Explain the benefit of SBP being 'indexed for inflation.'

**A:** The monthly payout increases with the annual Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA) to maintain purchasing power.

---

## Card 59

**Q:** What timeline is recommended for submitting a military retirement move request (HHG) via dps.move.mil?

**A:** As soon as retirement orders are received, ideally $4$ to $6$ months before retirement.

---

## Card 60

**Q:** What administrative step must be completed $60$ to $90$ days before retirement if a veteran is waiving military retired pay?

**A:** Send the waiver to the Retired Pay Operations Center of their specific service branch.
