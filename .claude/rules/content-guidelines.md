# Content Guidelines

## Audience & Tone
- Write for retired military veterans and federal employees
- Professional but approachable — not academic jargon
- Assume familiarity with military/federal service terminology (DFAS, VA, DoD, TRICARE)
- Do NOT assume financial expertise — explain concepts clearly
- Use direct language and actionable guidance

## Accuracy Standards
- All benefit amounts, eligibility rules, and regulations must be verifiable
- When citing specific dollar amounts or percentages, note they may change annually
- Include disclaimers: "Consult a qualified financial advisor for personal decisions"
- Reference official sources: VA.gov, OPM.gov, DFAS.mil, SSA.gov, Medicare.gov

## Content Structure Per Module
1. Module header with eyebrow label and descriptive title
2. Primary concept in a glass-panel with clear explanation
3. Supporting details in additional panels or 2-column grids
4. Pro tips in `.info-callout` blocks
5. Warnings in `.alert-warning` callout blocks
6. Feature lists for eligibility criteria or step-by-step items

## Key Acronyms (define on first use per module)
- BDD: Benefits Delivery at Discharge
- BRS: Blended Retirement System
- CRSC: Combat-Related Special Compensation
- CRDP: Concurrent Retirement and Disability Pay
- CSRS: Civil Service Retirement System
- FERS: Federal Employees Retirement System
- FEHB: Federal Employees Health Benefits
- FEGLI: Federal Employees' Group Life Insurance
- SBP: Survivor Benefit Plan
- TSP: Thrift Savings Plan
- DTI: Debt-to-Income ratio

## Topics to Never Include
- Specific investment product recommendations (individual stocks, funds)
- Tax filing advice (direct to CPA/tax professional)
- Legal advice (direct to attorney for estate planning)
- Political opinions on VA policy
